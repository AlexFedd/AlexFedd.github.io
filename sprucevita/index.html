Spruce Park opens to the public

A new park dedicated to spruce trees has opened to the public in San-Francisco, USA. The park, called Spruce Park, features a variety of spruce species, as well as other plants and animals that live in spruce forests. The park aims to educate visitors about the importance and benefits of spruce trees, as well as to provide a relaxing and enjoyable environment for nature lovers. Spruce Park covers an area of 10 hectares and has several attractions, such as a spruce museum, a spruce nursery, a spruce maze, a spruce playground, and a spruce cafe. The park also offers guided tours, workshops, and events related to spruce culture and history. The park is open from 9 am to 6 pm every day, and the entrance fee is 80 dollars for adults and 60 dollars for children.
